## Practitioner works

### Karl Wiegers, Joy Beatty — *Software Requirements*, 3rd ed., Microsoft Press, 2013

The global practitioner reference. Covers elicitation, analysis,
specification, validation, and management. Highly operational, with many
examples. Ideal for training a requirements team.

### Karl Wiegers, Candase Hokanson — *Software Requirements Essentials*, 2023

20 essential practices. A condensed, agile-compatible format. Good for a
quick audit of a requirements process.

### Suzanne & James Robertson — *Mastering the Requirements Process*, 4th ed., 2022

The **Volere** methodology. Open-source template, downloadable
(20,000+ downloads). Process-independent: agile, traditional, outsourced.
The reference for exporting/migrating between formats.

### Klaus Pohl — *Requirements Engineering: Fundamentals, Principles, and Techniques*, Springer, 2010 (rev. 2024)

The academic reference textbook. In-depth on elicitation, negotiation,
documentation, and validation. A long but educational read.

### Ian Sommerville, Pete Sawyer — *Requirements Engineering: A Good Practice Guide*, Wiley, 1997

Practical guidelines applicable to any project. Shorter than Pohl, more
prescriptive. Still relevant despite its age.

### Elizabeth Hull, Ken Jackson, Jeremy Dick — *Requirements Engineering*, 3rd ed., Springer, 2010

A systems-engineering approach. Excellent on **rich traceability**
(parent-child, derivation, satisfied-by). Suited to critical systems.

### AFNOR (collective) — *Expression du besoin et cahier des charges fonctionnel*, 2013

A direct application of NF EN 16271. Essential for French standards
conformance.

## Impact studies (key figures to cite)

- **56% of software defects** originate in the requirements phase (IBM SSI)
- **60% of rework costs** come from incorrect requirements (IBM SSI)
- **80% of project failures** have a requirements root cause (Standish CHAOS)
- **ROI of €3.30 to €7.50** per € invested in requirements engineering (Carnegie Mellon)
- **Total cost of poor software quality, US 2022: $2.41 trillion** (CISQ)

## How to cite in a professional CdC

Example of a *"Standards and methodological conformance"* section:

> *This cahier des charges is written in accordance with:*
>
> - *NF EN 16271 (February 2013) — Value management, functional expression
>   of the need and functional performance specification*
> - *ISO/IEC/IEEE 29148:2018 — Systems and software engineering, life
>   cycle processes, requirements engineering*
>
> *The functional-analysis methodology follows the APTE method.
> Requirements are written using the EARS pattern and prioritized
> according to the MoSCoW grid. Quality scoring conforms to the 9
> individual and 5 set characteristics defined by
> ISO/IEC/IEEE 29148:2018.*
