# Source Standards

## Required Source Mix

For a full report, use at least:

- one methodology source
- one sector or monetization benchmark
- one market/country/platform source when applicable
- one comparable company source when applicable
- one primary source whenever available

## High-Value Sources

Methodology:

- Strategyzer: Business Model Canvas, Value Proposition Canvas, Testing Business Ideas
- Bpifrance Creation: business model, Business Model Canvas, business plan
- Harvard Business Review / Steve Blank: Lean Startup, customer development
- Academic: Osterwalder/Pigneur/Tucci, Amit/Zott, Zott/Amit/Massa, Teece

Web/mobile monetization:

- RevenueCat State of Subscription Apps
- AppsFlyer app monetization reports
- OpenView, KeyBanc, Bessemer, Maxio for SaaS benchmarks
- Stripe docs and pricing
- Google Play payments policy
- Apple App Store Review Guidelines
- European Commission DMA, VAT and digital contract pages
- GSMA Mobile Money and World Bank Findex for payment/financial inclusion context

Comparable companies:

- SEC filings and annual reports for Dropbox, Duolingo, Spotify, Airbnb, Shopify, Roblox, Snap, Meta, Atlassian, GitLab, Unity, etc.
- Official pricing pages and developer docs.

## Scoring

Score sources on 100:

| Dimension | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Relevance to the project/model | 35 |
| Credibility and authority | 30 |
| Freshness for market/platform data | 20 |
| Information value and specificity | 15 |

Use:

- 85-100: strong, cite directly
- 70-84: useful with caveats
- 60-69: only for context or corroboration
- under 60: avoid unless explicitly flagged as weak

## Citation Rules

- Cite inline next to the claim.
- Use direct quotes sparingly; prefer paraphrase.
- Separate primary facts from interpretation.
- If benchmark data is category-specific, name the category.
- If the source is old but conceptual, state that it is used as a stable framework, not current market data.

## Red Flags

Avoid relying on:

- SEO blogs with no author or methodology
- "top business model" listicles without data
- vendor reports used to justify buying the vendor product unless corroborated
- outdated app store/payment rules
- anonymous market numbers
