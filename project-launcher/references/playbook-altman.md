# Sam Altman — Startup Playbook: key quotes by phase

Primary source: https://playbook.samaltman.com/ (~2015, still online)

Author: Sam Altman, former president of Y Combinator, CEO of OpenAI.

---

## Phase 0 — Idea

> "Your goal as a startup is to make something users love."

> "The startup graveyard is littered with people who thought they could skip this step."

> "The best ideas sound bad but are in fact good."

> "It's so much better if the idea comes first and the startup is the way."

> "The best answer is going after a large part of a small market."

**On assessing the market:** assess current size, growth rate, and ten-year trajectory. Favor the major technological shifts most people haven't yet seen.

**On secrecy:** don't obsess over it — good ideas don't get stolen. Execution is what counts.

---

## Phase 1 — User validation

> "Literally watch them use your product. Sit in their office if you can."

> "Value both what they tell you and what they actually do."

**Initial recruiting:** "Recruit users manually, and make the product so good the users recruit." Example, Ben Silbermann (Pinterest): he approached strangers in cafés in Palo Alto.

**PMF test (4 questions):**
- Are users using your product more than once?
- Are users fanatic?
- Would users be truly bummed if your company went away?
- Are users recommending you without asking?

---

## Phase 2 — Build & Iterate

> "If you improve your product 5% every week, it will really compound."

> "Faster iteration cycles correlate with better outcomes."

> "Do things that don't scale" — startup mantra.

> "Launch simple versions early; avoid big public releases."

**On quality:** "The best founders seem to care a little bit too much about product quality."

> "Remember, if you haven't made a great product, nothing else will save you."

---

## Phase 3 — PMF & Measurement

> "There is no other way to grow long-term sustainably." (than to have a product users love)

> "If you do not build a product users love you will eventually fail."

**On vanity metrics:** don't get fooled by signups. Focus on retention.

---

## Phase 4 — Growth & Execution

**Prime directive:** "Never lose momentum."

> "The company does what the CEO measures."

> "Two-word summary: focus and intensity."

> "Don't try to do everything—in fact, they say no a lot."

> "As a general rule, don't let your company start doing the next thing until you've dominated the first thing."

> "A very, very common cause of startup death is doing too many of the wrong things."

> "I have never, not once, seen a slow-moving founder be really successful."

**Success trap:**
> "Don't get caught up in early success."
> "Founders with publicists almost always fail."

**On competitors:**
> "Competitors are a startup ghost story."
> "99% of startups die from suicide, not murder."

**Displaying the NSM (Airbnb example):** posted on the fridge, above the desks, in the bathrooms.

---

## Team Phase — Hiring

> "Don't do it" (hire as late as possible).

> "Don't compromise on the quality of people you hire."

> "Companies that start with mediocre early employees almost never recover."

> "Cofounder breakups are one of the leading causes of death for early startups."

**Co-founder hierarchy:** good co-founder > solo > bad co-founder.

---

## CEO defaults

5 core responsibilities:
1. Set vision and strategy
2. Evangelize to everyone
3. Hire and manage team, especially in gap areas
4. Raise money
5. Set execution quality bar

> "Everything will feel broken all the time."

> "The CEO doesn't get to make excuses."

> "Building a company is somewhat like building a religion."

---

## Fundraising

> "The secret to successfully raising money is to have a good company."

> "Believe the no but not the reason."

> "Anything but yes is a no."

> "Insist on clean terms."
